About the Project

Visual and Sonic Landscapes of Muslims in Los Angeles is a research-driven project hosted by the Fowler Museum at UCLA.

Originally built with three disconnected datasets—sounds, places, and times—this redesign aims to weave those elements into a single, cohesive tapestry. By linking individuals to the locations they frequent and the eras they shaped, we provide a holistic view of the Muslim Angeleno experience.

The project utilizes archival photographs, community-led oral histories, and environmental sound recordings to create a multi-sensory digital archive.
